## Background and Opportunity

While decentralized finance has surpassed $100B in market capitalization, current DEX architectures face fundamental limitations that impede further market maturation and institutional adoption. Analysis of existing platforms reveals critical inefficiencies in both infrastructure design and market operation.

### Market Infrastructure Challenges

Fragmented liquidity across multiple protocols and chains
Inefficient capital utilization in traditional pool designs
Limited support for advanced trading strategies
Complex and costly trade execution processes

### Technical Limitations

Single-pool architectures restrict capital efficiency
Inadequate MEV protection mechanisms
High operational costs for liquidity providers
Insufficient risk management capabilities

### Institutional Requirements

Growing demand for professional trading infrastructure
Need for sophisticated execution capabilities
Increasing focus on compliance and security
Requirements for deep, unified liquidity access
